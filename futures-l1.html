A. Price Action & Trend Analysis

  • Look at the Chart: Observe the price chart. Identify trends: bullish (up) or bearish (down). In my first trade, I watched for a slight upward trend.
  • Support & Resistance Levels: Support is where price tends to stop falling; resistance is where price tends to stop rising. I paid attention to price bouncing between these levels.

B. Market Sentiment

  • Indicators: RSI, MACD, and Bollinger Bands help predict price movements. They show if market is overbought or oversold.

C. Technical Analysis Tools

  • Timeframes: Analyze price action on different timeframes (1, 5, 15 min). I used shorter timeframes to spot short-term trends, suitable for high leverage.
  • Moving Averages: SMA and EMA show trend direction. If price is above MA, likely an uptrend. Helped me decide when to enter first long position.
